Most people write the body first and slap a weak opener on it. That's backwards. Line 1 is 80% of the work.
Hook
Weird, specific, uncomfortable. Makes them stop. This is your only job on line 1.
Body
Value or story. Makes them care. Keep it tight — every sentence earns its place.
Close
Question or CTA. Makes them act. One thing only — never two asks.
Delete these on sight
"In today's world"
"Game-changer"
"Let's dive in"
"Delve into"
"Most captions are written from the inside out — what you want to say instead of what stops someone mid-scroll. If it doesn\'t connect in the first line, it's already lost."
Dan Guilfoil · Bluebird Co-founder

📌 The Meta Rule
Nobody generates fresh content from scratch for every platform. Start with one high-effort piece, then use AI to strip-mine it across formats. And always — paste a paragraph of your own writing into every prompt and tell Claude to match that tone. That single habit is the difference.
"Every prompt I build follows the same framework — Role, Context, Task, Format — and always ends with asking Claude to ask clarifying questions before it starts. That one habit alone made my outputs 10x more useful."
Emma Freeman · Bluebird Co-founder

For content creation, model choice matters. Here's exactly which one to use and why.
✅ Use this
claude-sonnet-4-20250514
Best voice-matching. Fast enough for real-time drafting. Significantly better creative quality than Haiku. This is your default for all content work.
Skip
claude-opus-4
More powerful but slower and more expensive. Overkill for captions and social copy. Save it for deep strategic work.
Skip
claude-haiku-4-5
Fastest and cheapest but noticeably weaker at voice-matching. The quality difference shows in content.
